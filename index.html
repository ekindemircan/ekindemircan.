Selected Work
Find Hidden Objects
The visual design of an existing mobile game was fully reworked to encompass newly added features while staying faithful to the game's core theme and color palette. Shop, Leaderboard, Achievements, and Gallery tabs were introduced as dedicated pages accessible via a persistent navigation bar — replacing a single main menu with a multi-tab structure for smoother, more intuitive navigation between sections. The redesign also targets a cleaner, more legible experience overall. Since this pattern closely mirrors how players already interact with similar games, long-time users are expected to adapt quickly and naturally.

About
Hi, I'm Ekin!

I started my journey in architecture, where I learned how people move through spaces and how design shapes experience. Along the way, I discovered a love for games — how they engage, challenge, and connect us. That curiosity eventually led me to UX design, where I found the perfect blend of my interests.

Now, I focus on designing user experiences for mobile games. I enjoy creating interfaces that feel intuitive, playful, and meaningful. My background helps me see design from both a creative and structured lens — and I love turning complex ideas into simple, fun interactions.

Process
How I design
01

Research & Map

Player flow analysis, competitor audits, and identifying friction points before any wireframe is drawn.

02

Wireframe

Lo-fi wireframes to align with product and engineering early — validate logic before investing in visuals.

03

High-Fidelity UI

Full Figma screens with auto-layout, components, and design tokens. Dev-ready in one clean handoff.

04

Test & Polish

Playtesting sessions, A/B reads, and data-driven iteration until the screen feels as good as it looks.
