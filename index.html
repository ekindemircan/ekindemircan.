About
Designing for
the player's emotion

I'm a UI/UX designer focused on mobile game interfaces — currently at a hidden objects & puzzle studio in Istanbul. I care about the small moments: the animation that rewards a correct tap, the popup that doesn't break immersion, the icon readable at any size.

My approach combines player psychology with systematic design. Every screen goes through user flow mapping, component design in Figma, and close iteration with the dev team — so what ships actually matches what was designed.

Outside work I follow the indie game scene, study behavioral economics, and collect typefaces.

Process
How I design
01

Research & Map

Player flow analysis, competitor audits, and identifying drop-off points before any wireframe is drawn.

02

Wireframe

Lo-fi wireframes to align with product and engineering early. Validate logic before investing in visuals.

03

High-Fidelity UI

Full Figma screens with auto-layout, components, and design tokens. Dev-ready in one clean handoff.

04

Test & Polish

Playtesting, A/B reads, and data-driven iteration until the screen feels as good as it looks.
